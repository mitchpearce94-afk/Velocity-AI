**Version:** 1.0

**Author:** Mitchell Pearce, Founder

## Executive Summary

Velocity AI is an AI operations agency that builds and deploys AI-powered systems for trade service businesses across Australia. We eliminate the manual admin burden — missed calls, slow quoting, poor follow-up — by deploying AI voice agents, automated workflows, and intelligent CRM systems for plumbers, electricians, builders, landscapers, and adjacent trades.

Our competitive edge is structural: the business itself runs on AI. Sage handles strategy, sales enablement, and content. Cortex handles code and deployment. Ava (our AI voice agent) runs outbound prospecting at scale. This means near-zero marginal labour cost per client, and the ability to scale acquisition volume without proportional headcount growth.

This plan lays out the path from 0 to 25+ active clients in 12 months, targeting $50K–$80K+ MRR by month 12 across three scenarios.

## 2. Target Market Analysis

### Primary Segments

**Tier 1 — High-value, high-volume trades (priority)**

- Plumbing companies (5–30 staff)
- Electrical contractors (5–30 staff)
- HVAC / air conditioning businesses
- Builders and construction project managers

These businesses have high call volumes, complex scheduling, and significant revenue per job ($500–$50K+). They feel the pain of missed calls and slow follow-up acutely.

**Tier 2 — Growth trades**

- Landscaping and garden maintenance
- Pest control
- Cleaning services (commercial)
- Roofing and guttering

Slightly lower revenue per job but high frequency. Good candidates for AI call handling and automated quoting.

**Tier 3 — Adjacent verticals (months 6+)**

- Property management companies
- Real estate maintenance divisions
- Franchise trade networks

### Market Sizing (Australia)

There are approximately 380,000 trade businesses in Australia. Roughly 60,000–80,000 have 5+ employees and sufficient revenue to afford our services. Our serviceable addressable market in the first 12 months (metro areas in NSW, VIC, QLD) is approximately 25,000–35,000 businesses. We need fewer than 0.1% market penetration to hit our Year 1 targets.

### Seasonal Considerations

Australian trade businesses have distinct seasonal patterns that affect both demand for our services and their willingness to invest:

- **October–November:** Peak season ramp-up. Trades are getting busy — this is when they feel the pain of missed calls most. Excellent selling window.
- **December–January:** Holiday slowdown. Many trades close or reduce hours over Christmas/New Year. Harder to sell, but existing clients still pay recurring fees. Pipeline building period.
- **February–March:** New year, new budgets. Many business owners make investment decisions for the year. Strong selling window.
- **April–September:** Steady state. Some trades (HVAC, plumbing) see seasonal spikes in winter. Generally consistent demand.

Our plan accounts for a ~40% reduction in new client acquisition during December–January.

## 5. Growth Levers

### 5.1 Referral Program

**Structure:** Offer existing clients $500 credit on their monthly invoice for every successful referral that signs up. This is cost-effective because the LTV of a referred client ($32K+ Year 1) far exceeds the referral bonus.

**Timing:** Launch formally in month 4, once there are enough active clients to seed the program.

**Target:** 15–25% of new clients from referrals by month 8.

**Mechanics:**
- Personalised referral links tracked in CRM
- Automated email to clients at the 30-day and 90-day mark asking for referrals
- Sage generates a monthly "client results" email that makes referral conversations easy ("Hey, Velocity just saved us 12 hours a week — you should check them out")

### 5.2 Trade Association Partnerships

Australia has numerous trade associations and industry bodies. These are high-leverage partnerships because they provide access to pre-qualified, trust-verified audiences.

**Target associations:**
- Master Plumbers Association (state chapters)
- Master Electricians Australia
- Housing Industry Association (HIA)
- Master Builders Association (state chapters)
- Landscape Queensland / Landscaping Victoria

**Partnership model:**
- Offer a co-branded "AI Operations Audit" as a member benefit (free)
- Present at association events and trade shows
- Negotiate preferred supplier status
- Contribute content to member newsletters

**Timing:** Begin outreach in month 3, aim for first partnership signed by month 5.

### 5.3 Supplier Network Partnerships

Trade suppliers (Reece, Tradelink, Bunnings Trade, Dulux Trade) have direct relationships with thousands of trade businesses. A referral arrangement or co-marketing deal with even one supplier could be transformative.

**Approach:** Position Velocity AI as a value-add that helps their trade customers run more professional businesses, which increases order volume for the supplier.

**Timing:** Exploratory conversations from month 6, once we have case studies and data to back the pitch.

### 5.4 Content and Authority Building

Sage generates ongoing content to build authority in the trades + AI space:

- Weekly LinkedIn posts from Mitchell's profile (industry insights, client wins, AI trends for trades)
- Monthly blog posts on the Velocity AI website (SEO-targeted)
- Quarterly case studies (with client permission) showing real ROI metrics
- Short-form video content: screen recordings of AI systems in action, before/after workflows

**Goal:** By month 6, Mitchell's LinkedIn should have 2,000+ connections in the Australian trades space, and the website should rank for 10+ long-tail keywords related to AI for trade businesses.

### 5.5 Service Expansion

Start with the core offering (AI call handling, workflow automation, CRM setup), then expand based on client demand:

| Timeline | Service Addition | Rationale |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Month 4–5 | AI-powered review management | Trades live and die by Google reviews |
| Month 6–7 | Automated quoting and job costing | High-value add for builders/electricians |
| Month 8–9 | AI receptionist + appointment booking | Natural extension of voice agent capability |
| Month 10+ | Full marketing automation (ads, retargeting) | Upsell to existing clients, higher MRR |

Each service expansion can increase MRR per client by $500–$1,500/mo without proportional cost increase.

### 5.6 Hiring Decisions

The AI-first model means hiring should be deferred as long as possible. The decision framework:

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| 15+ active clients, Mitchell spending >50% of time on delivery | Hire a part-time implementation specialist ($30–$40/hr contract) |
| 25+ active clients, consistent 5+ new clients/month | Hire a full-time operations/delivery person ($70–$90K salary) |
| 35+ active clients, strong pipeline | Consider a second salesperson or promote from within |
| 50+ active clients | Evaluate building a small team vs. staying lean with AI |

The key insight: every hire should free Mitchell to focus on sales and strategy, which are the highest-leverage activities. AI handles the rest.

## 7. Risk Assessment and Mitigation

### High-Impact Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Slow initial traction (month 1–3) | High | High | Use warm network for first 2–3 clients. Offer discounted setup for early adopters willing to be case studies. |
| AI voice agent quality issues | Medium | High | Extensive testing before launch. Human fallback for complex calls. Rapid script iteration based on call recordings. |
| Client churn due to poor onboarding | Medium | High | Standardise onboarding checklist. Weekly check-ins for first 30 days. Automated health-score monitoring. |
| Competitor entry (other AI agencies targeting trades) | Low–Medium | Medium | Move fast, build brand, lock in trade association partnerships. First-mover advantage in a niche market. |
| Seasonal cash flow crunch (Dec–Jan) | High | Medium | Build cash reserves in Q2–Q3. Ensure contracts have minimum 3-month terms. Focus on annual prepayment discounts. |
| Founder burnout (Mitchell doing everything) | Medium | High | AI handles 80%+ of operational work. Strict boundaries on working hours. Hire early if capacity is strained. |

### Contingency Plan

If growth is significantly below the conservative scenario by month 6 (fewer than 5 active clients):

1. Reassess the sales message — is the value proposition landing with trades?
2. Test a lower price point ($3K setup, $1K/mo) to reduce friction
3. Pivot outreach channel mix — if calls aren't working, double down on LinkedIn or partnerships
4. Consider offering a "pay for performance" model for 2–3 clients to build undeniable case studies
5. Explore adjacent verticals (dental, medical, professional services) if trades prove too resistant
