## 4. Onboarding Process

### Step 1: Discovery Call (15–30 minutes)
A no-obligation call with Mitchell to understand your business, your current setup, your pain points, and your goals. Mitchell will ask about your team size, call volume, current tools, and what's costing you time or money.

### Step 2: Custom Proposal (Within 48 hours)
Based on the discovery call, Mitchell puts together a tailored proposal outlining the recommended systems, expected outcomes, timeline, and investment. No generic packages — everything is built around your specific situation.

### Step 3: Onboarding Kick-Off
Once you're on board, we schedule a kick-off session to gather everything we need — business details, branding, service descriptions, scheduling preferences, access to existing tools, and any specific requirements.

### Step 4: Build & Configure (2–4 weeks)
We build and configure your AI systems. This includes AI training, integrations, testing, and quality assurance. You'll get progress updates throughout.

### Step 5: Review & Refine
Before going live, we walk you through everything, make any adjustments, and ensure you and your team are comfortable with how it all works.

### Step 6: Go Live
Systems go live. We monitor closely during the first two weeks to catch and fix any edge cases.

### Step 7: Ongoing Optimisation
We continuously monitor and improve your systems — tweaking AI responses, improving booking rates, and adapting to changes in your business.

**Total time from sign-off to go-live: typically 2–4 weeks** depending on scope.

## 5. Common Objections & Responses

### "It's too expensive"
Most of our clients were losing far more than $1,500 a month in missed calls alone. If your average job is worth $500 and you're missing just three calls a week, that's $6,000 a month walking out the door. The system usually pays for itself within the first month or two.

### "AI won't work for trades" / "My customers want a real person"
Our AI is specifically designed for trade businesses — it understands the language, the job types, and how to qualify leads properly. Most callers don't even realise they're talking to AI. And it's not replacing your team — it's catching the calls they can't get to when they're on a job.

### "I need to think about it"
Absolutely, take your time. But if it helps, the discovery call with Mitchell is completely free and no obligation. He'll give you a clear picture of what it'd look like for your business, and you can decide from there with all the facts. No pressure at all.

### "I'm too busy to set this up"
That's exactly why we handle everything. The onboarding process takes minimal time from you — about an hour or two total. We do all the heavy lifting. And once it's live, it actually frees up your time because the AI handles calls and bookings you'd normally have to deal with yourself.

### "I already have a website / CRM / system"
No worries — we're not about ripping out what works. A lot of our clients have bits and pieces already in place. Mitchell will look at your current setup and identify where the gaps are. Sometimes it's just one or two automations that make a huge difference.

### "How do I know this will work for my business?"
We only take on clients where we're confident we can deliver results. Mitchell will be upfront in the discovery call about whether it's a good fit. Plus, we don't do lock-in contracts — if it's not working, you're not stuck. But honestly, most clients see results within the first few weeks.

### "Can I see it in action?"
Mitchell can show you a live demo on the discovery call — you can actually ring the AI and test it yourself. It's the best way to see how natural and effective it is.

## 8. Industry-Specific Knowledge

### Common Trade Business Pain Points
- Missing calls while on the tools → lost jobs and revenue
- Admin overload → owner doing everything, working nights and weekends
- No-shows and late cancellations → wasted time and lost income
- Inconsistent follow-up → leads going cold, repeat business lost
- Disconnected tools → double entry, lost information, no visibility
- Can't scale → too reliant on the owner, can't grow without more staff
- Poor online presence → losing to competitors who show up on Google

### Common Tools Used by Trades
- **Job management:** ServiceM8, Tradify, Fergus, Simpro, AroFlo, Jobber
- **Accounting:** Xero, MYOB, QuickBooks
- **Scheduling:** Google Calendar, Outlook, Calendly
- **Communication:** Standard mobile, WhatsApp, Facebook Messenger
- **CRM:** Most have none, some use HubSpot or spreadsheets

### Terminology
- "On the tools" — physically doing trade work on-site
- "Tradie" — tradesperson
- "Sparky" — electrician
- "Chippy" — carpenter
- "Dunny" — toilet/bathroom (plumbing context)
- "Smoko" — short break
- "Arvo" — afternoon
- "Ute" — utility vehicle, common in trades
