# Velocity AI — Outbound Call Script

> Script for outbound cold calls to trade service businesses. Designed for Retell AI voice agent or human callers. The goal is to book a discovery call with Mitchell — not to sell on the phone.

---

## Key Principles

- **Value-first, not sales-first** — lead with the problem, not the product
- **Respect their time** — tradies are busy, keep it tight
- **Sound human** — not scripted, not corporate, not American
- **One goal only** — book the discovery call. That's it.
- **No pressure** — if they say no, be gracious. Leave the door open.
- **Australian English** — natural, conversational, relaxed

---

## Pre-Call Checklist

Before dialling, have these details ready (from lead list or CRM):
- Business name
- Owner/contact name (if available)
- Trade type
- Location
- Source (where we found them — Google, directory, referral, etc.)

---

## Script: Live Answer

### Opening (First 10 Seconds — Make or Break)

**If you have the owner's name:**
> "Hey [name], it's Ava from Velocity AI. Have you got thirty seconds? I'll be quick."

**If you don't have the name:**
> "Hey there, it's Ava from Velocity AI. I'm looking for the owner or whoever runs the show — is that you?"

**If they say they're busy:**
> "No worries at all — when's a better time to give you a quick ring? I literally just need two minutes."

**If they ask "What's this about?":**
> "Fair enough — we work with trade businesses like yours and I had a quick question. Are you still getting calls you can't pick up because you're on a job?"

---

### Quick Qualification (15 seconds)

Once you have their attention, qualify fast:

> "So [name], I work with Velocity AI — we help [trade type] businesses stop losing jobs to missed calls. Quick question: when a customer calls and you're on a job, what happens to that call right now?"

**Listen for responses:**

| Response | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| "Goes to voicemail" | Strong lead | Proceed to pain point discovery |
| "My wife/partner answers" | Moderate lead | Proceed — there may still be gaps |
| "We have a receptionist" | Lower priority | Probe: "And after hours or weekends?" |
| "We don't miss calls" | May not be a fit | Probe gently, then exit gracefully if confirmed |
| "Not interested" / hangs up | Not interested | Exit gracefully |

---

### Pain Point Discovery (30–60 seconds)

If they acknowledge missing calls or any frustration:

> "Yeah, that's super common in [trade type]. Most of the blokes we work with were in the same boat — flat out on the tools, calls going to voicemail, and by the time they call back, the customer's already rung someone else."

Then ask ONE of these follow-up questions (choose the most natural):

> "How many calls a week would you reckon slip through?"

> "What's the average job worth for you? Because even a couple of missed calls a week adds up fast."

> "Are you doing your own bookings and admin, or have you got someone handling that?"

---

### Brief Pitch (20–30 seconds)

Keep it short and tied to their specific problem:

> "So what we do is set up an AI phone system that answers every call to your business — even when you're on a job, after hours, weekends, whatever. It chats with the customer, qualifies the job, and books it into your calendar. You get a notification straight away. Most of our clients say they picked up at least three to five extra jobs a week just from calls they were already getting but couldn't answer."

**If they're engaged, add:**
> "And it's not just the phone system — we also help with booking automation, websites, CRM, the whole operations side. But the phone system is usually where the biggest wins are."

---

### Close for Discovery Call (The Ask)

> "Look, I'm not going to try to sell you anything on the phone — that's not how we work. But Mitchell, our founder, does a free fifteen-minute call where he looks at your setup and tells you exactly where you're losing jobs and what you could do about it. No obligation, no hard sell. Would you be up for a quick chat with him?"

**If yes:**
> "Legend. What day works best for you? Mitchell's usually free [suggest 2–3 times]. He can do early mornings before you head out, or after hours if that's easier."

Then collect:
- Preferred day/time
- Best phone number for the call
- Email for confirmation
- Anything specific they'd like to discuss

Confirm:
> "Spot on — I've got you down for [day] at [time]. Mitchell will give you a ring on [number]. You'll get a confirmation email at [email]. Cheers, [name] — you'll be glad you did."

**If "maybe" / "send me some info":**
> "Yeah no worries, I'll shoot you through some details. What's your email? And honestly, the info's useful but the best way to see if it's a fit is that quick call with Mitchell — he can actually show you a demo. Want me to tentatively lock in a time and you can always reschedule if you need to?"

**If "not interested":**
> "All good, [name] — appreciate your time. If things change down the track, we're always here. Have a good one."

## Script: Gatekeeper (Receptionist / Office Manager)

**Opening:**
> "Hey, it's Ava from Velocity AI. I was hoping to have a quick word with [owner name / the owner] — is [he/she/they] around?"

**If asked what it's regarding:**
> "We work with [trade type] businesses to help them stop losing jobs to missed calls. I just had a quick question for [him/her/them] — it'll literally take two minutes."

**If they offer to take a message:**
> "That'd be great, thanks. Can you let [him/her/them] know that Ava from Velocity AI called? We help [trade type] businesses catch every call even when the team's on a job — and I had a quick question about how [business name] handles after-hours calls. [He/She/They] can reach me at [callback number]. Cheers!"

**If they say the owner is unavailable:**
> "No worries at all. When's usually a good time to catch [him/her/them]? I'll try again then."

## Script: Voicemail

Keep it under 25 seconds. Speak clearly, naturally, and slightly upbeat.

> "Hey [name], it's Ava from Velocity AI. We work with [trade type] businesses and I had a quick question about how you're handling calls when you're on a job. Give us a ring back on [phone number] when you get a sec — or I'll try you again in a day or two. Cheers!"

**Alternative voicemail (value-hook version):**
> "Hey [name], Ava from Velocity AI here. I was chatting with a few [trade type — e.g., plumbing] businesses in [location] this week and most of them said they're losing three to five jobs a week just from calls they can't get to. We've got a fix for that — give us a ring on [phone number] if you want to hear about it. Cheers!"

## Handling Common Responses

### "How did you get my number?"
> "Your business came up when we were looking at [trade type] businesses in [location]. We work specifically with tradies, so we thought it'd be worth a quick chat. But if you'd rather we didn't call, no worries at all — I'll take you off the list."

### "We're not interested in AI"
> "Totally fair. The way we look at it, it's not really about AI — it's about making sure you don't lose jobs when you can't pick up the phone. Whether that's AI or a person doesn't matter, right? But look, I won't push it. If you ever want to have a look, just give us a ring. Cheers, [name]."

### "We're happy with our current setup"
> "That's great to hear — sounds like you've got things sorted. Out of curiosity, are you covered after hours and weekends as well? A lot of trades businesses are solid during the day but lose calls outside of business hours."

### "How much does it cost?"
> "Depends on what you need, but typically it's a one-off setup of around five to fifteen K and then fifteen hundred to three K a month for the ongoing management. Mitchell can give you an exact figure on the discovery call. Most of our clients recoup it within the first month from jobs they were previously missing."

### "I need to talk to my business partner"
> "Makes sense. Would it help if I booked Mitchell in for a call with both of you? That way you can both hear it at the same time and make a decision together."

### "Call me back another time"
> "No worries — when works best? I'll pop it in the diary and give you a ring then."

## Follow-Up Cadence

If no answer or no booking on first call:

| Attempt | Timing | Channel |
|---|---|---|
| 1st call | Day 0 | Phone (leave voicemail if no answer) |
| 1st follow-up | Day 2 | Phone (different time of day) |
| SMS | Day 3 | Text message (see template below) |
| 2nd follow-up | Day 5 | Phone |
| Email | Day 7 | Email (see template below) |
| Final attempt | Day 14 | Phone — last try, then move to nurture list |

## Disposition Codes

After each call, log the outcome:

| Code | Meaning | Next Action |
|---|---|---|
| **BOOKED** | Discovery call booked | Send confirmation, add to calendar |
| **CALLBACK** | Requested callback at specific time | Schedule follow-up |
| **VOICEMAIL** | Left voicemail | Follow cadence |
| **NO_ANSWER** | No answer, no voicemail | Follow cadence |
| **INTERESTED** | Interested but didn't book | Send info, follow up in 2 days |
| **NOT_INTERESTED** | Declined, not a fit | Move to nurture list, no further outbound |
| **DNC** | Asked to be removed from list | Remove immediately, log DNC |
| **WRONG_NUMBER** | Number incorrect or disconnected | Remove from active list |
| **GATEKEEPER** | Couldn't reach decision maker | Try again at different time |

## Compliance Notes

- Always identify yourself and the company at the start of the call
- If someone asks to be removed from the call list, do so immediately and confirm: "Done — you won't hear from us again. Sorry for the interruption."
- Respect the Australian Do Not Call Register — ensure lead lists are scrubbed before loading
- Calls to mobile numbers require prior consent under Australian spam regulations if using automated dialling. Verify compliance before outbound campaigns
- Never call before 9:00am or after 8:00pm local time (Australian Communications and Media Authority guidelines)
- Record calls only where legally permitted and with appropriate disclosure
