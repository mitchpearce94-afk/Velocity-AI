# Velocity AI — LinkedIn Outreach Templates

All messages written in Australian English. Keep them short, genuine, and zero corporate fluff. These are tradie-to-tradie conversations, not sales pitches.

---

## Connection Request Messages

Use these when sending the initial connection request. LinkedIn gives you ~300 characters, so keep it tight.

---

### CR-1: The Compliment Opener

> Hey [Name], saw your work at [Company] — love what you're doing in [their trade/location]. I work with trade businesses on streamlining their operations with AI. Always good to connect with sharp operators. Cheers, Mitchell

---

### CR-2: The Mutual Ground

> G'day [Name], noticed you're running a [trade] business in [city/region]. I come from the trades too — built my own security install company before starting Velocity AI. Always keen to connect with fellow business owners in the space.

---

### CR-3: The Specific Observation

> Hey [Name], saw your post about [specific thing — hiring, new van, project completion]. Scaling a trade business is no joke. I help tradies automate the backend so they can focus on growth. Would be good to connect.

---

### CR-4: The Problem Hook

> G'day [Name], quick question — are you still doing your quoting and scheduling manually? I work with [trade] businesses to automate the stuff that eats your evenings. Happy to share what's working if you're keen. Cheers.

---

### CR-5: The Social Proof

> Hey [Name], I built an AI operations system for my own trade business (Centrefit Group) and now I'm helping other tradies do the same. CRM, quoting, scheduling — the lot. Keen to connect and share some ideas. Mitchell

---

## Follow-Up Messages (2 Days After Connection)

Send these once they accept. Don't pitch hard — start a conversation.

---

### FU-1: The Value-First

> Thanks for connecting, [Name]. Out of curiosity — what's the biggest time-waster in your business right now? For most tradies I talk to, it's quoting and chasing follow-ups. We've been automating that stuff and the results have been pretty wild. Happy to share if you're interested.

---

### FU-2: The Quick Win

> Cheers for connecting, [Name]. Random thought — have you ever added up how many hours a week you spend on admin? Most trade business owners I talk to are sitting around 10–15 hours. We've been cutting that to under 2 with AI-powered automation. Let me know if you'd want to hear how.

---

### FU-3: The Peer Story

> Good to connect, [Name]. Just wrapped up a build for a security installation company — took their quoting from hours down to minutes and killed all the double-entry. If you're dealing with similar headaches, happy to walk you through what we did. No pitch, just a chat.

---

### FU-4: The Direct Offer

> Thanks for the connect. I'll keep it simple — I help trade businesses like yours set up AI-powered CRM, automated quoting, and scheduling systems. Built in 2 weeks, not months. If that sounds useful, happy to jump on a quick call. If not, no stress — good to be connected either way.

---

### FU-5: The Curiosity Play

> Cheers, [Name]. Quick one — if you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how your business runs day-to-day, what would it be? I ask because we build custom AI systems for tradies and I'm always curious what's top of mind.

## Nurture Messages (Warm Leads — Not Ready to Buy)

For people who've shown interest but haven't booked a call. Space these out over weeks. Keep the pressure low and the value high.

---

### NM-1: The Helpful Resource (1–2 weeks after last contact)

> Hey [Name], no pitch — just thought this might be useful. I put together a quick breakdown of the 5 biggest time-wasters I see in trade businesses and how to fix them. Want me to send it through? Figured it might resonate given what you mentioned about [their specific pain point].

---

### NM-2: The Industry Insight (2–3 weeks later)

> G'day [Name], just had a thought. One trend I'm seeing across trade businesses in [their area/trade] is that the ones picking up momentum fastest have all automated their quoting and follow-ups. Not saying you need to do anything right now — just interesting to see the shift happening. How's business going your end?

---

### NM-3: The Soft Re-engage (3–4 weeks later)

> Hey [Name], been a little while — hope the jobs are flowing. Still doing what we do at Velocity AI — setting up trade businesses with AI-powered operations. If anything's changed on your end or you want to have a yarn about what we could do for [Company], I'm around. No rush, no pressure. Cheers, Mitchell

## Usage Notes

**Personalisation is non-negotiable.** Every message should reference something specific about the person or their business. Their trade, their location, a recent post, a project they shared — anything that shows you've actually looked at their profile.

**Timing matters.** Send connection requests during business hours (7am–6pm AEST). Follow-ups should land mid-morning — after they've had their coffee but before they're on-site.

**Don't batch and blast.** LinkedIn will flag you if you send 50 identical messages. Vary the templates, personalise genuinely, and keep it to 15–20 connection requests per day max.

**Track everything.** Log each outreach in the CRM — who you contacted, which template, their response, next step. No point running outreach if you can't measure it.

**The goal isn't a sale.** The goal of LinkedIn outreach is to start a conversation. The sale happens on the discovery call. Don't try to close in a DM.
