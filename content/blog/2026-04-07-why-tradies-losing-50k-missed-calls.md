# Why Tradies Are Losing $50K+ a Year to Missed Calls

You didn't get into the trades to sit at a desk answering phones. You got into it because you're good with your hands, you like solving problems, and you wanted to build something for yourself.

But here's the thing — while you're up a ladder, under a house, or elbow-deep in a switchboard, your phone is ringing. And nobody's picking up.

That's not just an inconvenience. That's money walking out the door.

## The Maths Don't Lie

Let's keep this simple. Say you miss five calls a day. That's pretty conservative for a busy trade business — plumbers, sparkies, and builders we talk to often report missing ten or more.

Out of those five, let's say three are genuine enquiries (the rest are spam or suppliers). And let's say your average job is worth $500.

That's $1,500 a day in potential revenue you're not even getting a crack at.

Over a five-day work week? $7,500.
Over a year? **More than $390,000 in missed opportunity.**

Now, not every call converts. Fair enough. But even if you only close one in three, you're still looking at north of $130,000 a year — gone. Just because nobody picked up the phone.

Most trade business owners we speak to put the realistic figure somewhere between $50,000 and $100,000 a year. That's not small change. That's a new van. That's another apprentice. That's the difference between surviving and scaling.

## Why It Keeps Happening

It's not like you don't care. You know missed calls are a problem. But the reality of running a trade business makes it nearly impossible to answer every one.

**You're on the tools.** You can't drop everything mid-job to take a call. Your customers wouldn't want you to, either.

**Your team's the same.** If you've got a crew, they're just as flat out. Nobody's sitting around waiting for the phone to ring.

**After hours is a dead zone.** A huge chunk of calls come in after 4pm, on weekends, or first thing in the morning. That's when homeowners are home and thinking about the leaking tap or the dodgy power point. If you're not available, they're calling the next bloke on Google.

**Voicemail is a graveyard.** Let's be honest — when was the last time you left a voicemail and actually expected a call back? Your customers feel the same way. If they hit voicemail, most of them hang up and try someone else.

## Where All Those Leads Actually Go

Here's what stings: those missed calls aren't disappearing into thin air. They're going straight to your competitors.

The customer who needed a hot water system replaced today? They called you first, got no answer, and booked with the plumber down the road who picked up on the second ring.

The property manager with six units that need re-wiring? They left you a voicemail on Saturday. By Monday, they'd already signed with another sparky.

It's not that your competitors are better at the work. They're just easier to reach. And in the trades, being reachable is half the battle.

## So What's the Fix?

You've got a few options, and they all have trade-offs.

**Hire a receptionist.** Works well, but you're looking at $50-60K a year in wages, plus super, leave, and all the rest. For a lot of trade businesses, that's hard to justify — especially when call volume isn't consistent.

**Use a virtual answering service.** Better than voicemail, but you're still paying per call, and the person on the other end doesn't know your business. They're reading a script, not booking jobs.
