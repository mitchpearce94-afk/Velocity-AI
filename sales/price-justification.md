# Velocity AI — Price Justification & ROI Ammunition

**Purpose:** When a prospect questions the price, you don't defend — you reframe. Every number below is designed to make $699–$2,499/mo feel like a rounding error compared to what they're losing right now.

---

## The Core Reframe

Never justify the price against "software." Justify it against what they're currently paying — in money, time, and lost revenue — to NOT have this system.

> "It's not a cost. It's the gap between where you are and where you should be."

## Objection-Specific Ammunition

### "It's too expensive"
**Calculate it live on the call:**
> "You said you're spending about [X] hours a week on admin. At your charge-out rate of $[Y]/hr, that's $[Z] a month in time alone — before we even count the missed calls and lost quotes. Our system costs [$tier]. You're already spending more than that doing nothing about the problem."

### "$699 is a lot for a small business"
> "I get it. But think about it this way — that's $23 a day. Less than a tradie's smoko run. And it's answering your phone 24/7, following up your quotes, and booking your jobs while you sleep. What's one missed job worth?"

### "I can get a CRM for $49/mo"
> "You can. And you'll spend 6 months setting it up, it won't answer your phone, it won't follow up your quotes, and it won't send review requests. You'll be back to doing admin at 9pm because the tool only does 20% of what you need. We do 100% — and we set it up for you."

### "Let me think about it"
> "No worries. But let me ask — if this was going to save you 10 hours a week and bring in an extra $3K–$5K a month in jobs you're currently missing, what's the cost of waiting another month to start?"

### "Can you do a discount?"
> "The pricing reflects what it costs us to build and maintain a system this comprehensive. What I can do is start you on Starter at $699 and upgrade you to Professional once you've seen the results. That way you're not committing to the full package until you know it works."
