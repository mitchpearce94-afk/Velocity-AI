# Velocity AI — Discovery Call Script

**Duration:** 30–45 minutes
**Caller:** Mitchell Pearce, Founder
**Purpose:** Understand the prospect's current operations, surface pain points, paint the vision, and book them into a build slot.

---

## Pre-Call Prep (5 mins before)

Before you pick up the phone:

- Check their website, Google reviews, and socials — know what trade they're in, how many staff, and where they operate.
- Look at their current tech stack if visible (Tradify, ServiceM8, Fergus, spreadsheets, etc.).
- Note anything personal — recent award, new van wrap, team milestone — use it in rapport.
- Have pricing tiers open but don't lead with them.

---

## Phase 1: Rapport & Context (5 mins)

The goal here is simple — be a normal human, not a salesperson. You're a tradie who built tech. Lean into that.

**Opening:**

> "Hey [Name], Mitchell here from Velocity AI. Thanks for jumping on — I know you're probably between jobs so I'll make sure this is worth your time. How's the week been?"

Let them talk. Match their energy. If they're flat out, acknowledge it. If they're relaxed, take your time.

**Transition into context:**

> "Sweet. So just to set expectations — this isn't a pitch. I just want to understand how you're running things at the moment and see if what we do is actually a good fit. If it's not, I'll tell you straight. Sound fair?"

**If they ask "what do you do?" early:**

> "Short version — we set up AI-powered operations for trade businesses. CRM, quoting, scheduling, marketing, the lot. But the specifics depend on what you actually need, so let me ask you a few things first."

---

## Phase 2: Problem Dig (10–15 mins)

This is where the money is. Don't rush it. Every question should make them feel the pain of their current setup.

### Current Operations

> "Talk me through a typical day. A job comes in — what happens from the moment the phone rings to when you get paid?"

Listen for: manual steps, double-entry, things falling through cracks, bottlenecks.

> "What are you using to manage jobs and customers right now?"

Follow-ups based on answer:

- **Tradify/ServiceM8/Fergus:** "How's that going for you? What's the biggest thing it doesn't do well?"
- **Spreadsheets/paper:** "How much time would you reckon you spend on admin each week? Honestly."
- **Nothing formal:** "So it's mostly in your head and your phone? How often does something get missed?"

### Quoting

> "How long does it take you to get a quote out after a site visit?"

> "What's your follow-up process look like? Do you chase them up or just hope they come back?"

> "Roughly, how many quotes would you lose in a month because you were too slow or forgot to follow up?"

### Scheduling & Team

> "How do you manage your team's schedule? Who decides who goes where?"

> "When something changes — cancellation, emergency job — how does that get communicated?"

> "Do your guys have visibility on what's coming up, or are they waiting for you to tell them?"

### Marketing & Lead Gen

> "Where are most of your jobs coming from right now? Word of mouth, Google, socials?"

> "Do you do any follow-up marketing to past customers? Like reminders for annual servicing, that sort of thing?"

> "If you could wave a magic wand and have a steady flow of the right kind of jobs, what would that look like?"

### The Money Question

> "If you added it all up — the hours on admin, the missed quotes, the jobs you didn't follow up — what do you reckon that's costing you? Per week? Per month?"

Let them do the maths. Don't fill the silence. This is the moment they sell themselves.

---

## Phase 3: Vision & Aspiration (5 mins)

Shift from pain to possibility. Get them excited.

> "If we could take all that admin off your plate — the quoting, the scheduling, the follow-ups — and it just happened automatically, what would you do with that time?"

> "Where do you want the business to be in 12 months? More revenue? More staff? Less stress? Or all three?"

> "What would it mean for you personally if the business could run properly without you being across every single thing?"

---

## Phase 4: Solution Presentation (5–7 mins)

Now — and only now — you talk about Velocity AI. Tailor it to the problems they've just told you about.

**Framework:**

> "Based on everything you've told me, here's what I'd build for you..."

Map each solution to a specific pain they raised:

| They Said | You Say |
|---|---|
| "Quoting takes forever" | "We'd build you a quote wizard — your guys pick the products, it calculates everything, and the customer gets a professional PDF in minutes, not days." |
| "I forget to follow up" | "Automated follow-up sequences. Every lead gets chased — texts, emails — without you lifting a finger." |
| "Scheduling is chaos" | "Drag-and-drop scheduling board. Your team sees their day on their phone. Changes sync in real time." |
| "No repeat business marketing" | "We set up automated campaigns — service reminders, seasonal offers, review requests — all running in the background." |
| "I can't see how the business is going" | "Live dashboards. Revenue, jobs completed, quote conversion rate — all in one place." |
| "Phones ring and nobody answers" | "AI voice agent picks up, qualifies the lead, books the job — 24/7. No missed calls." |

**Key differentiators to weave in:**

- "We do this in 2 weeks, not 2 months."
- "Everything's built specifically for trades. No generic SaaS rubbish."
- "We did this for my own business first — Centrefit Group. It's not theory, it's proven."
- "Transparent pricing. No hidden fees, no lock-in contracts."

---

## Phase 5: Pricing & Close (5–7 mins)

**Introduce pricing naturally:**

> "So in terms of investment, we've got three tiers. Based on what you've described, you'd probably sit in the [Starter/Professional/Enterprise] tier."

| Tier | Setup | Monthly | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $2,500 | $699/mo | Owner-operators, 1–3 staff |
| Professional | $5,000 | $1,499/mo | Growing teams, 4–10 staff |
| Enterprise | $10,000 | $2,499/mo | Multi-team operations, 10+ staff |

> "The setup fee covers your custom build — CRM, quoting, scheduling, automations, voice agent — everything configured specifically for your business. We split it 50/50, so half upfront and half when you're live. The monthly covers the platform, AI, and ongoing support. No lock-in. And if you go annual, we'll knock 10% off."

**Quick reference — what each tier includes (use to tailor the pitch):**

- **Starter:** AI Voice Agent (inbound, 200 mins/mo), basic CRM, automated quote follow-up, Google review requests, 1x onboarding session, email + chat support.
- **Professional:** Everything in Starter + inbound & outbound AI voice (500 mins/mo), full CRM with pipeline, automated quoting with branded PDFs, smart scheduling, marketing automation, live dashboard, 3x onboarding sessions, priority support, monthly strategy call.
- **Enterprise:** Everything in Professional + unlimited AI voice, multi-location, custom integrations, advanced reporting, compliance workflows, subcontractor management, dedicated account manager, weekly strategy calls.

**Anchor to their pain before price lands:**

> "You mentioned earlier you're losing roughly [their number] a month in missed quotes and admin time. Most of our clients see ROI within the first 4–6 weeks just from the quoting automation alone."

**Trial close:**

> "Does that sit roughly where you expected?"

**If positive:**

> "We've got [X] build slots open for [month]. If you're keen to lock one in, I'll send through a payment link for the first half of the setup fee and the onboarding form today. We'll have you up and running in two weeks. Want to lock it in?"

**If they hesitate on the setup fee:**

> "Think of it this way — if you hired an agency to build a custom CRM, quoting system, and marketing automation, you'd be looking at $15K–$25K minimum. We're doing the whole lot for a fraction of that because we've built the framework already. Your setup fee covers the custom configuration for your specific trade and business."

**If they need to think:**

> "No dramas. I'll send you through a summary of what we discussed and the one-pager with pricing. What day works to have a quick follow-up — Thursday or Friday?"

Always book the next step. Never leave it as "I'll get back to you."

## Objection Handling

### "It's too expensive"

> "I hear you. Let me ask — you mentioned earlier you're probably losing [amount they estimated] a month in missed quotes and wasted admin time. If we can claw even half of that back, you're ahead from month one. This isn't a cost — it's the engine that accelerates your growth."

If they push back harder:

> "Look, you can keep paying your current 'cost' in lost time and missed revenue, or you can invest in the system that fixes it. The Starter tier is $699 a month — less than a day's work for most tradies. And it saves you hours every single week. The setup fee is split 50/50 so you're not out of pocket for the full amount upfront."

If they compare to cheaper software:

> "ServiceM8 or Tradify will run you $50–$100 a month, sure. But you still need to set it up yourself, it doesn't do your quoting properly, there's no AI answering your phones, and you've got zero marketing automation. When you add up what you'd pay separately for a CRM, a call answering service, an email marketing tool, and a quoting system — you're easily at $2,000–$3,000 a month. We're doing it all for less, and it's actually built for your trade."

### "I'm not techy"

> "Neither are your customers, and they'll be using the quoting portal no worries. We build everything to be dead simple — if you can use a smartphone, you can use this. Plus, we handle the setup, the training, and the ongoing support. You don't need to be techy. That's literally why we exist."

### "I tried software before and it didn't work"

> "Which one? [Let them answer.] Yeah, I hear that a lot. The problem with most software is it's built for everyone and works for no one. We build specifically for your trade, your workflow, your team. And because we set it up for you — not just hand you a login — it actually gets used. We've done this with Centrefit Group and the difference is night and day."

### "I need to talk to my partner/wife/business partner"

> "Absolutely. Would it help if I jumped on a quick 10-minute call with both of you? I can answer any questions they've got and make sure everyone's on the same page. When would suit?"

### "Can I see a demo?"

> "100%. I'll send you through a walkthrough of the Centrefit build — real system, real data, real results. Have a look and we'll jump on a quick follow-up after. Sound good?"

### "What's the guarantee?"

> "Simple. If we don't deliver what we promised in the two-week build window, you don't pay. We also do monthly — no lock-in contracts. If it's not delivering value, you can walk. But honestly, no one does, because it works."

### "I'm too busy right now"

> "That's exactly why you need this, mate. You're too busy because you're doing everything manually. The best time to fix that was six months ago. The second best time is now. We handle the build — all you need is a couple of hours across two weeks for input and training."

## Post-Call Actions

Immediately after hanging up:

1. **Log the call** — key pain points, tier discussed, next step, timeline.
2. **Send follow-up email** within 1 hour — thank them, summarise what you discussed, attach the one-pager.
3. **Book the follow-up** — if they didn't commit, get it in the calendar before they go cold.
4. **Add to nurture sequence** — if they're not ready now, they go into the email sequence.
5. **LinkedIn connect** — if not already connected, send a personalised request referencing the call.

---

## Key Reminders

- **Listen more than you talk.** The ratio should be 70/30 in their favour during the problem dig.
- **Use their words.** When you present the solution, mirror the exact language they used to describe their problems.
- **Don't oversell.** If they only need Starter, don't push Professional. Trust builds referrals.
- **Be honest.** If Velocity AI isn't the right fit, say so. It builds more credibility than any sales technique.
- **You're not selling software.** You're selling time back, money back, and peace of mind.
