# Velocity AI — Trade-Specific Discovery Questions

**Companion to:** discovery-call-script.md
**Purpose:** Deep-dive questions tailored to each trade. Use during Phase 2 (Problem Dig) after the universal questions. These surface the pain points that are specific to their trade — the ones that make them think "this bloke actually gets what I do."

**How to use:** After the general operations/quoting/scheduling questions, pick 4–6 from their trade section. Don't read them like a script — weave them into the conversation naturally. The goal is to get them talking about the stuff that keeps them up at night.

---

## Plumbing

### Emergency vs Scheduled Work
- "What's your split between emergency callouts and scheduled work? Roughly what percentage?"
- "When an emergency comes in at 6pm, what happens to tomorrow's schedule? How do you shuffle things around?"
- "Do you have a dedicated after-hours line, or is it your mobile ringing at 2am?"

### Compliance & Paperwork
- "How are you handling compliance certificates right now? Are they going straight to the council or are they piling up?"
- "Gas compliance — is that something you're staying on top of easily, or is it one of those things that slips?"

### Cash Flow Killers
- "How many hot water replacements do you quote per week? What's your close rate on those?"
- "When a drain camera job turns into a bigger relining job, how quickly can you get that revised quote out?"
- "Warranty callbacks — are you tracking those properly, or do they just eat into your schedule unplanned?"

### Growth Blockers
- "If you had a system that answered every call, booked the job, and sent the confirmation — would you be turning work on, or are you already at capacity?"
- "Are you losing emergency jobs to competitors because no one picks up the phone fast enough?"

### What to listen for:
- Missed after-hours calls = AI voice agent
- Compliance paperwork stacking up = automated certificate generation
- Slow quoting on hot water/relining = quote automation
- No follow-up on quoted jobs = nurture sequences
- Can't handle more volume = operations overhaul, team scheduling

## Electrical

### Compliance & Certificates
- "How are you managing your electrical safety certificates? Paper forms, an app, or something else?"
- "When a certificate needs to go to the QBCC or the council, what's that process look like? Is it smooth or a nightmare?"
- "RCD testing for commercial clients — are you doing reminder campaigns for annual testing, or waiting for them to call?"

### Solar & Battery
- "Are you doing solar installs? What percentage of your work is solar vs general electrical?"
- "How do you handle the CEC paperwork and STC claims? Is that streamlined or a pain point?"
- "For solar quotes — are you doing site assessments then quoting manually, or do you have a calculator?"

### Job Complexity
- "Switchboard upgrades — how long between the initial enquiry and actually getting on site? What's slowing it down?"
- "When you quote a job and it turns out the wiring needs more work than expected, how do you handle the variation?"
- "Do you track which jobs end up being more profitable than others? Like, do you know if commercial or residential makes you more money per hour?"

### Team & Apprentices
- "How many apprentices have you got? Who manages their training and sign-offs?"
- "When you send an apprentice with a licensed sparky, how do you make sure the paperwork reflects who did what?"

### What to listen for:
- Certificate management chaos = automated compliance workflows
- No RCD reminder system = recurring revenue campaigns
- Manual solar quoting = quote configurator
- Apprentice management headaches = job allocation and training tracking
- No visibility on job profitability = reporting dashboard

## Building / Construction

### Project Coordination
- "How many projects are you running at any one time? And how do you keep track of where each one is at?"
- "Subcontractor scheduling — what's your system for making sure the right trade shows up on the right day?"
- "When a subbies doesn't show or runs late, how does that cascade through your schedule?"

### Money & Claims
- "Talk me through your progress claim process. How often are you lodging claims, and how long does it take to get paid?"
- "Variations — are they getting documented properly on site, or do they turn into arguments at the end of the job?"
- "How are you tracking costs against budget during a build? Do you know if you're on track or only find out at the end?"

### Compliance & Council
- "Council inspections — how do you manage those bookings and make sure they happen at the right stage?"
- "When you get a defect list at handover, how do you track and close out each item?"
- "Are you handling your own DA submissions, or outsourcing that?"

### Client Communication
- "How are you keeping clients updated during the build? Regular updates, or do they just call you constantly?"
- "Do you have a system for before-and-after photos and progress documentation?"

### What to listen for:
- Subcontractor scheduling chaos = drag-and-drop scheduling with notifications
- Slow progress claims = automated invoicing tied to project milestones
- Untracked variations = digital variation orders with sign-off
- No real-time cost tracking = project budget dashboard
- Client communication overload = automated progress updates

## HVAC / Air Conditioning

### Seasonal Demand
- "How do you handle the pre-summer rush? Are you turning work away, or are you trying to capture all of it?"
- "Do you do any proactive outreach before the season hits — like reaching out to last year's customers?"
- "What happens to your team between seasons? Is there enough maintenance work to keep everyone busy?"

### Maintenance Contracts
- "Are you doing maintenance contracts? What percentage of your revenue is recurring vs one-off installs?"
- "How do you track which units are due for service? Is it a spreadsheet, a system, or just memory?"
- "When a warranty is about to expire on a unit, do you reach out to the customer to lock in an extended service plan?"

### Technical & Quoting
- "For new installs, how are you sizing units? Manual load calcs, or do you have software?"
- "Split system vs ducted — do you quote both and let the customer choose, or recommend one? How long does that process take?"
- "Parts ordering — when you need a specific part mid-job, how quickly can you source it? Is that ever a bottleneck?"

### Compliance
- "How are you handling your F-gas compliance and refrigerant tracking?"
- "Energy efficiency certificates — are those easy to produce, or another bit of paperwork?"

### What to listen for:
- No proactive seasonal outreach = automated marketing campaigns
- Maintenance contracts on spreadsheets = CRM with automated reminders
- Manual load calculations = quote configurator
- Parts sourcing delays = supplier integration
- No warranty expiry tracking = automated upsell campaigns

## Roofing

### Storm & Insurance Work
- "When a big storm hits, how do you handle the surge? Do you have a system to triage and prioritise?"
- "Insurance jobs — what's your process for dealing with assessors? Is that smooth, or does it slow everything down?"
- "After storm season, how many leads do you lose because you couldn't get back to them fast enough?"

### Safety & Compliance
- "Height safety compliance — are you managing that in-house? How do you track harness inspections, training expiry?"
- "Safe Work Method Statements — are you creating those from scratch each job, or do you have templates?"
- "When you've got multiple crews on different roofs, how do you make sure everyone's covered?"

### Estimation & Materials
- "How accurate are your material estimates? How often do you end up short or over-ordering?"
- "For re-roofing jobs, are you doing the measurements manually on site or using satellite tools?"
- "Colorbond vs tile — do you track which is more profitable for you?"

### Documentation
- "Before-and-after photos — are you taking those consistently? Where do they live?"
- "When a customer calls back 6 months later about a leak, can you quickly pull up what was done?"

### What to listen for:
- Storm surge overwhelm = AI call handling + automated triage
- Insurance process friction = document management and assessor communication
- Lost leads during busy periods = automated follow-up
- Safety compliance tracking gaps = digital compliance management
- No job history lookup = CRM with photo documentation

## Landscaping

### Design to Build
- "Do you do the design in-house, or do you bring in a landscape architect? How does that handover work?"
- "When a design changes mid-project — and it always does — how do you manage the cost and timeline impact?"
- "What's your quoting process like for a full landscape build? How long from site visit to quote sent?"

### Maintenance & Recurring Revenue
- "Are you doing ongoing maintenance for past clients? What does that look like?"
- "How do you manage maintenance schedules — mowing, hedging, seasonal planting? Is it all in your head?"
- "If a maintenance client wants additional work — new garden bed, irrigation fix — how does that get captured?"

### Seasonal Planning
- "How does your workload shift through the year? What's your quiet period?"
- "Do you do any proactive outreach before spring — like contacting past clients about garden refreshes?"
- "Irrigation setups before summer — is that a big part of your business? How do you manage those bookings?"

### Suppliers & Materials
- "How do you handle plant ordering from nurseries? Any issues with availability or lead times?"
- "For hardscape materials — pavers, stone, timber — do you carry stock or order per job?"

### What to listen for:
- Design change chaos = digital variation management
- No recurring maintenance system = CRM with automated scheduling
- Seasonal quiet periods = marketing automation to fill gaps
- Manual quoting = landscape quote builder with material calculator
- Supplier coordination issues = order management system

## Painting

### Estimation & Quoting
- "How do you estimate a job? Are you measuring up manually or using any tools?"
- "What's your accuracy like? How often does a job end up taking longer than you quoted?"
- "Do you break down your quotes by room or give a lump sum? What works better for conversions?"

### Scheduling & Weather
- "Exterior work — how do you manage weather disruptions? What does that do to your schedule?"
- "When rain pushes a job back, how do you communicate that to the customer and reorganise?"
- "How far ahead are you booked? And how do you manage the waitlist when you're full?"

### Colour & Client Management
- "Colour consultations — do you offer that, or do clients come to you knowing what they want?"
- "How often do clients change their mind on colour mid-job? How do you handle that?"
- "Sample pots and test patches — is that part of your process, or does it depend on the client?"

### Commercial & Strata
- "Do you do body corporate or strata work? How does that pipeline work — is it tender-based?"
- "For commercial repaints — shopping centres, offices — how do you manage after-hours access and scheduling?"
- "Multi-stage jobs — primer, undercoat, topcoat across multiple areas — how do you track what's done?"

### What to listen for:
- Inaccurate estimates eating margin = measurement and estimation tools
- Weather schedule chaos = smart scheduling with weather integration
- No waitlist management = automated booking and pipeline management
- Colour change friction = digital colour selection workflow
- Multi-stage tracking gaps = job progress tracking per area

## Concreting

### Quoting & Measurement
- "How are you measuring up jobs? Tape measure and calculator, or do you have something more?"
- "For a standard driveway or slab, how long between enquiry and quote sent?"
- "Do you include 3D renders or visual mock-ups in your quotes, or is it just a written description?"

### Scheduling & Weather
- "Concrete is weather-sensitive — how do you manage pours around rain forecasts?"
- "When a pour gets pushed back, how do that ripple through your other bookings?"
- "How far in advance do you book your concrete deliveries? Any issues with timing?"

### Team & Equipment
- "How many crews are you running? Who coordinates which crew goes where?"
- "Equipment maintenance — concrete pumps, bobcats — how do you track servicing?"
- "When you need extra hands for a big pour, how do you source them? Labour hire, subbies?"

### Client Communication
- "Do you send progress updates during a job? Photos of the prep, the pour, the finish?"
- "How do you handle customer expectations around curing time and when they can use the surface?"
- "Warranty and defect management — if a slab cracks, how do you handle that process?"

### What to listen for:
- Slow quoting = automated measurement and quote generation
- Weather coordination chaos = smart scheduling with weather alerts
- No progress documentation = automated photo and update system
- Equipment tracking gaps = asset management system
- Labour coordination issues = crew scheduling platform

## Universal Pain Point → Solution Map

Use this to connect what you hear to what you sell, regardless of trade:

| Pain Point Signal | What They Say | What You Sell |
|---|---|---|
| Missed calls | "Phone rings off the hook" / "Can't answer when I'm on the tools" | AI voice agent — 24/7 call answering |
| Slow quoting | "Takes me days to get quotes out" / "I do them at night" | Quote automation / wizard |
| No follow-up | "I just hope they come back" / "Don't have time to chase" | Automated email/SMS sequences |
| Admin overload | "I'm doing invoices at 9pm" / "Weekends are for paperwork" | Full operations automation |
| Lost leads | "Probably losing a few a week" / "Don't know how many I miss" | CRM + lead pipeline tracking |
| Team chaos | "They don't know what's happening till I tell them" | Smart scheduling + mobile app |
| No marketing | "All word of mouth" / "Don't have time for marketing" | Automated campaigns + review requests |
| No visibility | "Don't really know my numbers" / "Find out at tax time" | Live dashboards + reporting |
| Compliance gaps | "Certificates pile up" / "I should be better at that" | Digital compliance workflows |
| No recurring revenue | "Every month starts from zero" | Maintenance contracts + automated reminders |

## Red Flags (Not a Good Fit)

If you hear any of these, be honest and don't force it:

- Owner-operator with 0 staff doing <$150K revenue — too small, won't see enough ROI at $499/mo
- Already on Tradify/ServiceM8 AND happy with it — they don't need another system
- "Just looking for a website" — that's not what we do, refer them
- Resistant to tech entirely — "I've done it this way for 30 years" — they won't adopt
- Cash business avoiding records — compliance risk, walk away

## Key Metrics to Extract (for your pitch)

Try to get actual numbers during the call. These become your ammunition in the close:

1. **Missed calls per week** — even 3/week × average job value = massive annual loss
2. **Time on admin per week** — usually 10–15 hours, × their hourly rate = real cost
3. **Quote conversion rate** — most tradies don't know this, which proves they need tracking
4. **Average time to send quote** — if >24 hours, they're losing jobs
5. **Revenue per employee** — benchmark against industry average for their trade
6. **Percentage of repeat customers** — if <30%, there's a retention problem
7. **Number of Google reviews** — if <50, there's a reputation problem

These numbers become the ROI calculation in Phase 4.
